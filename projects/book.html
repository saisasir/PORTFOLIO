A workspace booking system for the MIT Libraries.

Overview

School libraries often have workspaces that serve as great study spots. During peak hours, however, finding an open space can be time and energy-consuming. In order to make finding a place to study less stressful for students, I conceptualized MITbook, a workspace reservation app for easy availability checking and booking in my school's libraries.

User research and synthesis

To build a better understanding of how students view workspaces in the university libraries and how they approach finding one, I conducted 10 one-on-one interviews with MIT students. When recruiting research participants, I was mindful of getting a diverse range in class years and frequency in which they use the libraries.

From the interviews, it became evident that students using library workspaces largely fell under one of three categories: those doing individual work who prefer to sit alone, those doing individual work who prefer to sit with friends, and those doing group work. Ideation

Based on the findings, I defined my project goal: an app that allows busy students, who have varying needs and preferences, to intuitively find and secure library workspaces. I then drew up a storyboard for how MITbook could fit in to address some frustrations, and started thinking about the app's structure and actions.

Creating a booking

Users can specify their preferences for the booking such as library, floor, workspace type (individual or group), and duration, as well as features like wheelchair-accessible, whiteboard availability, and noise level. If they choose a group workspace, they are given additional options for making the group public so that others can join. Additional fields only become visible once the user chooses to create a public group, so as to not clutter the screen for other users. Once they confirm their preferences, the system will find an open workspace that best matches, which users can review before making the booking.

Checking in

On the home screen, users can check their upcoming bookings. At the start of the allotted time, they are required to check in to confirm that they're there. If 15 minutes pass without a check-in, the space will be released for others to book and use.
